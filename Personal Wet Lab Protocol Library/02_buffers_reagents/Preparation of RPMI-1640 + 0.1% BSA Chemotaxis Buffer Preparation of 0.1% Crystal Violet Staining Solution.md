# Standard Protocol 1: Preparation of RPMI-1640 + 0.1% BSA Chemotaxis Buffer

**Document type:** Laboratory standard protocol  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [24/03/2026]  


---

## 1. Purpose

To prepare **50 mL sterile RPMI-1640 + 0.1% BSA buffer** for use in chemotaxis assays, including Boyden chamber / transwell migration experiments.

---

## 2. Scope

This protocol applies to preparation of a **serum-free, antibiotic-free chemotaxis buffer** from **RPMI-1640 medium** and **BSA powder**, followed by **0.22 µm sterile filtration** before use on cells.

---

## 3. Principle

BSA is added to serum-free RPMI-1640 to provide a defined low-protein environment suitable for migration assays. Because BSA powder and bench-top handling are not sterile, the final buffer must be **sterile-filtered** before use in cell culture experiments.

---

## 4. Safety and Handling

- Use only **plain RPMI-1640** without FBS, Pen/Strep, glutamine supplements, or other additives unless specifically required by the experimental design.
- BSA powder is lightweight and may be affected by static electricity; handle gently during weighing.
- Do **not** vortex vigorously after BSA addition, as excessive foaming may interfere with dissolution and filtration.
- Perform sterile filtration in a **biosafety cabinet (BSC)** using sterile supplies.
- Follow local institutional biosafety and waste disposal regulations.

---

## 5. Reagents and Materials

### Reagents
- RPMI-1640 medium, **plain / unsupplemented**
- BSA powder (Bovine Serum Albumin, typically Fraction V)
- 70-75% ethanol for surface disinfection

### Consumables
- Weighing paper or weighing boat
- Spatula
- Two 50 mL conical tubes
- One new sterile 50 mL conical tube
- Sterile disposable syringe (20 mL or 50 mL, no needle)
- Sterile **0.22 µm syringe filters**, preferably **PES**
- Laboratory marker / label tape

### Equipment
- Analytical balance
- Biosafety cabinet (BSC)

---

## 6. Calculation

### Target formulation
**0.1% w/v BSA in RPMI-1640**

This means:

- **0.1 g BSA / 100 mL**
- Therefore, for **50 mL**, weigh:

\[
0.1 \text{\[
0.1 \text{ g} \times \frac{50}{100} = 0.05 \text{ g}
\]

**Required BSA = 0.05 g = 50 mg**

---

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Bench preparation and dissolution
1. Prepare all materials on a clean laboratory bench.
2. Confirm that the RPMI bottle is **plain RPMI-1640 only**, with **no FBS** and **no antibiotics** added.
3. Place weighing paper or a weighing boat on the analytical balance and tare.
4. Accurately weigh **50 mg BSA powder**.
5. Add approximately **40 mL RPMI-1640** into a clean 50 mL conical tube.
6. Carefully transfer the weighed **50 mg BSA** into the tube containing RPMI.
7. Close the cap securely.
8. Dissolve the BSA by **gentle inversion** or **slow circular swirling**.  
   Do **not** vortex vigorously.
9. Allow the tube to stand at room temperature for approximately **5 minutes** if needed, then continue gentle mixing until the solution appears homogeneous.
10. Once the BSA is fully dissolved and no visible particles remain, bring the total volume up to **50 mL** with additional plain RPMI-1640.

---

### 7.2 Sterile filtration in the biosafety cabinet
1. Spray all required items with 70-75% ethanol and transfer them into the BSC:
   - prepared RPMI + BSA solution
   - one new sterile 50 mL conical tube
   - sterile syringe
   - sterile 0.22 µm syringe filter(s)
2. Assemble the syringe and attach a **0.22 µm sterile syringe filter** securely.
3. Transfer a portion of the unfiltered buffer into the syringe.
4. Hold the filter outlet over the new sterile 50 mL tube.
5. Slowly and evenly press the plunger to filter the solution into the sterile receiving tube.
6. Repeat until the full **50 mL** has been filtered.

### Important note
If filtration becomes difficult because of protein-related resistance:
- Do **not** force the plunger excessively.
- Replace the clogged filter with a new sterile 0.22 µm filter and continue.

---

## 8. Labeling

Label the final sterile tube clearly with:
- **Chemotaxis Buffer (RPMI + 0.1% BSA)**
- **Filtered 0.22 µm**
- date
- initials

### Example
`Chemotaxis Buffer (RPMI + 0.1% BSA), filtered 0.22 µm, [date], [initials]`

---

## 9. Storage and Use

- Store at **4°C**
- Before use, allow the buffer to equilibrate to **room temperature for approximately 30 minutes**
- Use within a time frame consistent with local laboratory practice and sterility standards

---

## 10. Quality Control / Acceptance Criteria

The preparation is acceptable if:
1. Correct BSA mass (**50 mg**) was used
2. Final volume is **50 mL**
3. BSA is fully dissolved
4. The solution has been **sterile-filtered through 0.22 µm**
5. The final tube is correctly labeled and stored at **4°C**

---

## 11. Troubleshooting

### BSA does not dissolve easily
- Mix gently by inversion or swirling
- Allow a short standing time at room temperature
- Avoid vigorous vortexing to reduce foam formation

### Excessive foam forms
- Let the tube rest until bubbles dissipate
- Resume gentle mixing only

### Syringe filter clogs
- Replace with a new sterile filter
- Do not apply excessive force

---

## 12. Summary

This protocol yields **50 mL sterile RPMI-1640 + 0.1% BSA chemotaxis buffer** suitable for migration assays after **0.22 µm sterile filtration**.

# Standard Protocol 2: Preparation of 0.1% Crystal Violet Staining Solution (500 mL)

**Document type:** Laboratory standard protocol  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [24/03/2026]  


---

## 1. Purpose

To prepare **500 mL of 0.1% crystal violet staining solution** for staining migrated cells in transwell / Boyden chamber assays.

---

## 2. Scope

This protocol applies to preparation of a **0.1% crystal violet solution in 20% alcohol / 80% water** for routine laboratory staining applications.

---

## 3. Principle

Crystal violet is dissolved in an aqueous alcohol solution to generate a stable staining reagent. Because commercial crystal violet powder may contain insoluble particulate material, final filtration is recommended to improve staining quality and reduce visible debris on membranes or microscopy preparations.

---

## 4. Safety and Handling

- This solution does **not** need to be sterile.
- Preparation must be performed in a **chemical fume hood** if **methanol** is used.
- Crystal violet powder stains skin, gloves, benchtops, and clothing very easily.
- Methanol is **toxic** and **flammable**.
- **Absolute ethanol** may be used instead of methanol if required by local laboratory practice.
- Wear appropriate PPE, including gloves and lab coat, and follow institutional chemical safety procedures.

---

## 5. Reagents and Materials

### Reagents
- Crystal violet powder
- Methanol **or** absolute ethanol
- Deionized water / ddH2O / Milli-Q water

### Consumables
- Weighing paper or weighing boat
- Spatula
- Filter paper (recommended)
- Label tape
- Aluminum foil

### Equipment
- Analytical balance
- Chemical fume hood
- 100 mL graduated cylinder
- 500 mL graduated cylinder
- 500 mL glass bottle (e.g., Schott bottle)
- Magnetic stirrer
- Stir bar
- Funnel

---

## 6. Calculation

### Target formulation
**0.1% w/v crystal violet in 500 mL total volume**

This means:

- **0.1 g / 100 mL**
- Therefore, for **500 mL**:

\[
0.1 \text{ g} \times \frac{500}{100} = 0.5 \text{ g}
\]

**Required crystal violet = 0.5 g**

### Solvent composition
Final solvent system:
- **20% methanol (or absolute ethanol)**
- **80% water**

For **500 mL total volume**:
- **100 mL methanol or ethanol**
- **400 mL water**

---

## 7. Procedure

### 7.1 Weighing
1. Place weighing paper or a weighing boat on the balance and tare.
2. Carefully weigh **0.5 g crystal violet powder**.
3. Minimize air disturbance during weighing, as the powder is lightweight and stains easily.

---

### 7.2 Preparation of solvent
1. In a chemical fume hood, measure **100 mL methanol** using a graduated cylinder.  
   If methanol is not used, measure **100 mL absolute ethanol** instead.
2. Transfer the alcohol into a **500 mL glass bottle**.
3. Measure **400 mL deionized water** using a graduated cylinder.
4. Add the water to the same bottle.
5. Mix gently.

---

### 7.3 Dissolution of crystal violet
1. Add the weighed **0.5 g crystal violet powder** to the bottle containing the solvent mixture.
2. If powder adheres to the bottle neck or funnel, rinse it down using a small amount of the solvent already in the bottle.
3. Add a clean stir bar to the bottle.
4. Place the bottle on a magnetic stirrer.
5. Stir at **moderate speed** for **30-60 minutes**, or until the solution appears uniformly dark purple.

---

### 7.4 Filtration (recommended)
1. After mixing is complete, prepare a clean receiving bottle or clean the original bottle if transferring back later.
2. Place a funnel with **standard filter paper** over the receiving bottle.
3. Slowly pour the crystal violet solution through the filter paper.
4. Allow filtration to proceed by gravity until complete.

### Rationale
This step removes insoluble particles that may otherwise deposit on transwell membranes or interfere with microscopy and cell counting.

---

## 8. Labeling

Label the final bottle clearly with:
- **0.1% Crystal Violet**
- **in 20% methanol**  
  or  
  **in 20% absolute ethanol**
- intended use (optional), e.g. **For Transwell Staining**
- hazard warning: **Toxic / Flammable**
- date
- initials

### Example
`0.1% Crystal Violet (in 20% absolute ethanol), For Transwell Staining, Toxic / Flammable, [date], [initials]`

---

## 9. Storage

- Store at **room temperature**
- Keep the bottle **tightly closed**
- Protect from prolonged light exposure by wrapping with **aluminum foil** or storing in a dark location

---

## 10. Quality Control / Acceptance Criteria

The preparation is acceptable if:
1. Correct crystal violet mass (**0.5 g**) was used
2. Final volume is **500 mL**
3. Solution appears homogeneous after stirring
4. Filtration was performed if particulate contamination is a concern
5. Bottle is clearly labeled with composition, hazard information, and date

---

## 11. Troubleshooting

### Undissolved particles remain after stirring
- Extend stirring time
- Filter the solution before storage

### Visible debris appears during staining
- Re-filter the solution through fresh filter paper

### Strong odor / vapor exposure during preparation
- Confirm that preparation is being performed in a functioning **chemical fume hood**

---

## 12. Summary

This protocol yields **500 mL of 0.1% crystal violet staining solution** in **20% methanol or 20% absolute ethanol**, suitable for routine transwell staining applications.
