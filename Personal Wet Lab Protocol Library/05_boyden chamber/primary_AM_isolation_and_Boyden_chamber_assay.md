# Isolation of Primary Alveolar Macrophages from Mouse BALF and Boyden Chamber Chemotaxis Assay

## 1. Overview

This protocol describes the isolation of primary alveolar macrophages (AMs) from mouse bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF) and their subsequent use in a Boyden chamber chemotaxis assay.

The workflow includes:

1. Collection of BALF from mouse lungs  
2. Filtration, centrifugation, and washing of BAL-derived cells  
3. Cell counting and viability assessment using Trypan Blue  
4. Short equilibration of freshly isolated primary AMs  
5. Boyden chamber setup with chemoattractants  
6. Fixation, crystal violet staining, membrane drying, mounting, imaging, and quantification  

This protocol is intended for trained researchers and should be optimized according to the specific experimental model, mouse strain, animal age, reagent source, and institutional requirements.

---

## 2. Safety and ethical notes

All animal procedures must be performed only by trained personnel under an approved institutional animal protocol and according to local animal welfare regulations.

The exact anesthesia, euthanasia, and BALF collection procedures must follow the approved local animal protocol. This document does not provide animal anesthesia dosing instructions.

Chemical safety rules should be followed when handling DMSO, paraformaldehyde, crystal violet, and other reagents.

---

## 3. Principle

Alveolar macrophages are located in the alveolar space and can be collected by repeated bronchoalveolar lavage. The recovered BALF is filtered to remove debris, centrifuged to pellet cells, and washed into the appropriate assay buffer.

For the Boyden chamber assay, freshly isolated primary AMs are loaded into the upper chamber of a transwell insert. Chemoattractants are placed in the lower chamber. Cells that migrate through the porous membrane toward the chemoattractant are fixed, stained, imaged, and quantified.
